Top Cybersecurity Tips

1: Vary up your passwords

Hackers have a method of obtaining your password called a dictionary attack, where a program they make will scan through all possible combinations until it finds the one you use. In order to circumvent this, it is best to choose a password that is a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. This makes it more difficult for their program to figure it out. Also be sure to always use a different one for each website you visit.

2: Enable a Firewall

Firewalls block malicious information from being downloaded from the internet. It acts as a filter and ensures that the only data coming in is authorized and safe.

3: Monitor Your Digital Footprint

Your digital footprint is your history of activity on the internet. By keeping track of the websites you visit, where you give your information to, and what you click on, you can trace back anything malicious that affects your computer in order to take the appropriate action. Everything you post and type on social media is able to be traced to you as well. It is important to be careful of what you say, as even if deleted, it can be retrieved and pulled back up.

4: Beware of Phishing Scams

Phishing is when someone disguises as something trustworthy in order to obtain your personal information. This is often in the form of emails and phone calls. Every time you receive an email that looks slightly off, check the sending address. Often times it will be incorrect or part of it will be different. For example, a legit Paypal email is from service@paypal.com, and will have the proper company header. Never click on suspicious links or trust anything that you feel seems unsafe.

5: Use Public Wi-Fi Cautiously

Public wifi networks use a type of encryption called WPA2, which has very easy security flaws that hackers can exploit. This allows them to intercept your information and obtain whatever you are doing. For this reason, it is important to try and avoid using public wifi networks, and if you absolutely must, do not make purchases or use anything that requires the entry of sensitive information. Another option is using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) to add another layer of security, but these are expensive and often times do not help much.
